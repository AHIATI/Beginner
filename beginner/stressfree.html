What is Stress?

Stress is a natural physical and mental response to a perceived threat or demand. It's a normal part of life, and can be caused by a wide range of factors, including: Internal factors: worries, fears, anxiety, or pressure to meet goals or expectations External factors: work, relationships, finances, health, or environment When we experience stress, our body's "fight or flight" response is triggered, releasing stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. This can lead to physical symptoms like: Rapid heartbeat, Sweating, Tense musclesHeadaches Fatigue And emotional symptoms like: Anxiety Irritability Mood swings Difficulty concentrating Feeling overwhelmed Chronic stress can have negative effects on our overall well-being, increasing the risk of: Mental health conditions like depression and anxiety disorders Physical health problems like hypertension, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease Weakened immune system Digestive issues Sleep disturbances Recognizing the sources of stress and learning effective coping strategies can help manage stress and improve overall quality of life.
