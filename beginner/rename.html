CAUSES OF STRESS

"Stress is like a master puzzle-maker, weaving together a complex web of factors that can leave us feeling overwhelmed and stuck. But what are the pieces that make up this puzzle? What are the hidden triggers, subtle saboteurs, and overt obstacles that contribute to our stress levels? Let's take a closer look at the fascinating, yet frustrating, causes of stress - from the internal whispers of self-doubt to the external pressures of modern life. By understanding the sources of stress, we can begin to dismantle its hold on our lives and start building a more resilient, stress-free future." This can be grouped into Internal and External causes. You can read more on our youtube channel

  1. Internal Causes

    2. Internal causes of stress can have a profound impact on our well-being, and include negative thought patterns, unmanaged emotions, personality traits like perfectionism and competitiveness, physical health issues, sleep disturbances, and lack of purpose. These internal causes, such as catastrophic thinking, anxiety, anger, fear, chronic illness, pain, discomfort, insomnia, and feeling unfulfilled and uncertain about one's direction in life, can interact and exacerbate one another, creating a complex web of stress that can be challenging to navigate.This can be summarised into:

    • Negative Thought Patterns
    • Unmanaged Emotions
    • Personality Traits
    • Physical Health

  2. External causes

    3. External causes of stress can have a significant impact on our well-being, and include work-related stressors such as long hours, heavy workload, office politics, and lack of control, financial stressors like debt, financial insecurity, and unexpected expenses, relationship stressors such as conflicts, communication breakdowns, social isolation, and difficult or toxic relationships, and environmental stressors like noise, pollution, clutter, lack of natural light, and uncomfortable or unsafe living conditions. These external causes can be unpredictable and outside of our control, making them even more challenging to manage, and can interact with internal causes to create a complex web of stress that affects our overall health and well-being.External causes can also be summarised into:

    • Work-Related Stressors
    • Financial Stressors
